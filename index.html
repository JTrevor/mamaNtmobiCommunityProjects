About

A little information about who we are and where it all started

  • Easter 2007

    Our Humble Beginnings

    MNCP starts of as a ministry of The Christian Fellowship in the adjacent Jika Joe Settlement. As we meet under a tree for shelter from the rain or sun, we call this our Wayside Sunday School.

  • 2008

    We begin growing!

    Mrs. Joyce Dlamini, who was Pastor Chris’ domestic helper visited the Wayside Sunday School at Jika Joe and pleaded with us todo something for the children in her community at the Ezinketheni Informal Settlement (which was formally known as Mattisons).  Calvin and Yolland Chetty started off the second informal settlement ministry in 2008.

  • 2016

    Where are we now

    We care for over 450 different young children from a number of settlements. We have a number of MNCP volunteering members who either have full time jobs, are students or pensioners

  • 2017 and beyond

    The Future is Bright for our communities

    With help from volunteers, sponsors and other generous people, we continue to grow. We are continually expaning our reach in the hope for better communities

Education

Our main focus of areas in Eduation are

Lifeskills programme for children aged between 2 and 18.
Career Guidance
Teaching Extra Lessons (English) at Umsilinga Primary School.
Early Childhood Education as there are currently no crèches or preschools in Ezinketheni
Home visits to identified vulnerable children at risk
Visits to the schools they attend to ensure that they receive all the help and support to excel academically
Financial Assistance for school uniforms, shoes, bags and stationery.
Providing research material for school assignments
Reading and Book Clubs
